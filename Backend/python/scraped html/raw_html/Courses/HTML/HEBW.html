Hebrew
  

  • HEBW 100-Jewish Humor

    (3 units)

    An introduction to Modern Jewish Studies through an exploration of modern Jewish humor, with a particular focus on minority identity, assimilation and acculturation, and the way Jewish humor was shaped by and in turn shaped American culture.

    Both grading options. Same course as HIST 140 . Not repeatable for credit. Not open to credit for those with existing credit in HIST 140 .

  

  • HEBW 101A-Fundamentals of Hebrew

    (4 units)

    Designed for non-native speakers, introduces students to Hebrew and enables them to comprehend and communicate in Hebrew at increasingly complex levels.

    Both grading options. For non-native speakers only. Not open to heritage speakers or native speakers of Hebrew.

  

  • HEBW 101B-Fundamentals of Hebrew

    (4 units)

    Continuation of HEBW 101A . Introduces students to Hebrew and enables them to comprehend and communicate in Hebrew at increasingly complex levels. For non-native speakers only. Not open to heritage speakers or native speakers of Hebrew.

    Both grading options.

  

  • HEBW 210-Love and Marriage in the Biblical Tradition

    (3 units)

    An introduction to Jewish literature on love and marriage using the Bible and associated literature to explore the tradition and its values.

    Letter grade or credit/no credit
